# Sergey Levine

Sergey Levine is a professor at UC Berkeley and a foundational figure in robot learning and reinforcement learning. As co-founder and Chief Scientist of Physical Intelligence, he bridges academic research with commercial robotics development.

## Background

- **Position**: Professor, UC Berkeley (EECS Department)
- **Ph.D.**: UC Berkeley, under Pieter Abbeel
- **Previous**: Google Brain research scientist

## Academic Contributions

### Reinforcement Learning Pioneer
- Recognized as a pioneer in applying deep reinforcement learning to robotics
- Developed algorithms enabling robots to learn complex manipulation tasks
- Key work in learning from demonstration, self-supervised learning

### Key Research Areas
- Deep reinforcement learning for control
- Learning from visual observations
- Multi-task and transfer learning for robots
- End-to-end robot learning

## Open Source Leadership

### Contributions
- Key figure in [[octo]] development
- Collaborated on [[open-vla]] through Berkeley + Stanford partnership
- Extensive open-source code releases for the research community

### Academic-Industry Bridge
- Work spans academic research and practical deployment
- Emphasis on making robotics research reproducible
- Community building through open tools

## Commercial Role

### Physical Intelligence
- Co-founder and Chief Scientist
- Leads technical direction of π₀ development
- Brings research expertise to commercial product development

### "Avengers League"
As one of the key figures who moved from Google's closed-source projects (RT-1, RT-2) to open-source work and then commercial entrepreneurship, Sergey represents the pattern of:

1. **Google era**: Built foundational robot models (RT-1, RT-2)
2. **Open source era**: Contributed to Octo, OpenVLA
3. **Commercial era**: Co-founded PI, leads π₀ development

## Research Philosophy

Key insights from his work:
- **Data efficiency**: Robots can learn from relatively small datasets with right approach
- **Generalization**: Multi-task and diverse data enable better transfer
- **End-to-end**: Learning perception, reasoning, and action jointly

## Student Success

As advisor to [[chelsea-finn]] (his PhD student), Sergey has helped train the next generation of robotics researchers. This student-advisor relationship working together at PI represents a unique collaboration in the field.
